PEANUTS

Peanuts yellowish orange pea-like flowers are borne in axillary clusters on the stems above ground and last for just one day. Following self-pollination, the flowers fade and wither. The stalk at the base of the ovary, called the pedicel, elongates rapidly, forming a "peg" that grows downward into the soil.

Once firmly buried, peg elongation stops and the tip swells to form the fruit. For harvesting, the entire plant, including most of the roots, is removed from the soil. The fruits have wrinkled shells that are constricted between pairs of the one to four (usually two) seeds per pod.
